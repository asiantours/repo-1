The Pearl of the Orient

The Philippines, a small nation with 7, 641 islands, is diverse in cultural heritage and nature. About 3.5 million tourists travel in the country every year, visiting infamous destinations, such as beaches, mountains, diving spots, islands and rainforests. Filipinos speak Tagalog, as their main dialect, and English, as the second language. To learn more about the Philippines, click here.

Tourist Spots

  • Boracay, Western Visayas

    • It is a small island in the Philippines, and it is known for its infamous resorts and beaches. Offshore, coral reefs and shipwrecks are home to diverse marine life. A trip to the beach is not your basic sun tanning and swimming - there is more to that! You can go island hopping with your friends, ATV riding, parasail to feel like a bird, scuba dive, swim with the whalesharks, and be a mermaid. Yup, you heard it, mermaid. They have mermaid tail rentals, that you can use for photoshoots, and lessons that they offer. For Boracay's accomodations, click here.





  • Vigan Historic City, Vigan

    • A historical city that treasures the traditional infrastructures during the Spanish colonial era. It has many baroque-styled Cathedrals, and traditional Filipino vintage houses that were influenced by the Spaniards. You can observe weavers weave abel cloth - a tradition that is passed on to generations. Vigan also has antique souvenirs you can take home aside from the abel cloth - vintage jewelry, furniture, keychains, and more! For accomodations, click here.

Food Trip

Cuisines Description
Halo-Halo You will find yourself sweating and craving for a cold dessert in the hot Philippines, and halo halo, will satisfy you. It is a dessert made up of shaved ice and evaporated milk that is then added with various ingredients such as kidney beans, flan, ice cream, agar jelly, and fruits.
Lechon In the Philipinnes, celebrating milestones aren't complete without lechon. Lechon is a whole pig that is roasted, thus requiring a big group to feast on it.
Balut Thrills don't only mean heights, but also food! Balut is a duck embryo that is boiled and eaten from the shell.
