The Pearl of the Orient

The Philippines, a small nation with 7, 641 islands, is diverse in cultural heritage and nature. About 3.5 million tourists travel in the country every year, visiting infamous destinations, such as beaches, mountains, diving spots, islands and rainforests. Filipinos speak Tagalog, as their main dialect, and English, as the second language. To learn more about the Philippines, click here.

Tourist Spots

  • Boracay, Western Visayas

    • It is a small island in the Philippines, and it is known for its infamous resorts and beaches. Offshore, coral reefs and shipwrecks are home to diverse marine life. A trip to the beach is not your basic sun tanning and swimming - there is more to that! You can go island hopping with your friends, ATV riding, parasail to feel like a bird, scuba dive, swim with the whalesharks, and be a mermaid. Yup, you heard it, mermaid. They have mermaid tail rentals, that you can use for photoshoots, and lessons that they offer. Be mezmerized as unique and intricate sandcastles are formed right in front of you! At night, local fire dancers illuminate the dancefloor. For Boracay's accomodations, click here.





  • Vigan Historic City, Vigan

    • A historical city that treasures the traditional infrastructures during the Spanish colonial era. It has many baroque-styled Cathedrals, and traditional Filipino vintage houses that were influenced by the Spaniards. You can observe weavers weave abel cloth - a tradition that is passed on to generations. Vigan also has antique souvenirs you can take home aside from the abel cloth - vintage jewelry, furniture, keychains, and more! Try their longganisa, a sausage influenced by the Spanish, and experience the Longganisa Festival. For accomodations, click here.





  • Puerto Princesa Subterranean River National Park, Palawan

    • Declared as on of the seven wonders of the world and a UNESCO World Heritage Site, Puerto Princesa's underground river should be one of your stops. On a boat with your friends, a tour guide will lead you to see the unique formations of the cave, and bats that inhabit it. This site has a second floor, meaning that there are small waterfalls inside the cave. Be amazed by the wonder of the world's longest underground river. For accomodations, click here

Food Trip

Cuisines Description
Halo-Halo Do you recall the slushies you would get in carnivals? Do you think it tastes pretty good? Your opinion will change when you give it a shot for halo-halo. Imagine Halo-halo as slushie's cool sibling. Halo-halo is not only composed of shaved ice and evaporated milk, but has ice cream, mung beans, flan, agar jellies, wafer sticks - anything you can think of. Well, the literal meaning of "halo-halo" is actually "mix-mix" in Filipino, which means that all the sweet flavours will mix in a cup, big or small, to satisfy you in a hot day!
Lechon In the Philipinnes, celebrating milestones aren't complete without lechon. Lechon is a whole pig that is roasted, and this is influenced from the Spaniards. But don't ever think of eating a whole pig by yourself - have a big group of people to feast it on with! However, Filipinos do not only eat the pig, but pair it with some sweet sauce, rice, and sometimes other food.
Balut Imagine an ordinary duck egg. Not bad right? Now, as you crack it open, you see a duck embryo in it. You heard it, duck embryo. Yes, it may be gross to hear about, but it is actually considered as a delicacy in the Philippines. If you consider yourself a a daredevil, you must prove yourself to be a real one by trying this grotesque delicacy.
